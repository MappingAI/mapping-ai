An interactive stakeholder map of the American AI landscape. Open data. Built together.

Why this exists

AI policy moves fast and the landscape is hard to see

Hundreds of people and organizations are shaping how AI gets built and governed in America. Who they are, what they believe, and how they're connected is scattered across news articles, LinkedIn profiles, congressional testimony, and institutional memory.

We're trying to put it all in one place.

What we built

mapping-ai.org

An interactive force-directed graph of the U.S. AI policy ecosystem. People, organizations, and resources linked by affiliations, funding, collaboration, and authorship. Filterable by category, searchable by name, plottable by regulatory stance and AGI timeline beliefs.

Where we need help

The data is messy and the tool has rough edges

  • 44% of entities have no notes at all
  • Some notes contain AI-generated fabrications that look plausible
  • The UI has bugs we haven't found yet
  • The map could do more (timelines, shareable links, better plots)
  • Nobody outside this room knows about it
  • The data tells stories we haven't explored
